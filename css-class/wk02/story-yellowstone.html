The Road to West Yellowstone

by Erick Patino

The morning began with a delicious French toast breakfast in the cozy cabin. The air was filled with the sweet aroma of cinnamon and maple syrup, making it the perfect way to fuel up for the day ahead. I was excited to set out on my adventure to West Yellowstone for the first time, and as I got ready, the cool mountain air felt refreshing and slowly warmed as the sun climbed higher into the sky.

The drive itself was peaceful, with the road winding through the towering pines of Island Park, where the trees seemed to stretch endlessly into the horizon. As I neared the entrance to Yellowstone, the anticipation grew. However, upon arriving, we ran into a long line of cars, all inching their way toward the park's entrance. It was hard to be patient, but the promise of Yellowstone's wonders made it all worth it.

Finally, after what seemed like an eternity, we entered the park to find the most amazing views that were instantly breathtaking. It captured an endless green landscape filled with bubbling geysers and beautiful scenery. One of the first marvels I saw was the boiling pots, with steam rising and a distinct smell, almost like rotten eggs. The colors were clear blues and greens.

I knew this would be a trip to remember and one of the most incredible, unique experiences.
